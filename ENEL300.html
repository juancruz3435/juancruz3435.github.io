ENEL 369 Reflection

How well you know your computer? "Very well" used to be my answer, after finishing ENEL 369 I realised how little I actually knew. We dove into the foundations of how a computer interprets a command given by the user and the process of executing that command. This was by far the most interesting course for me.


Takeaways from the course:

  • • I enjoyed learning how to program the AVR128DB28 chip.
  • • We utilized the Analog Comparator (AC), the Analog to Digital Converter (ADC), and Digital to Analog Converter (DAC) peripherals in the AVR chip to convert and compare analog/digital signals.

    • Applications:

    In my groups ILS project we used the AVR to compare an analog signals from a microphone to a reference voltage. Our intention with this was to have the microphone pick up the sound of our hands clapping, which normally output a voltage of 1V, and depending on the number of claps (ranging from 1 to 3) the AVR would turn up, down, or mute the volume respectively.
